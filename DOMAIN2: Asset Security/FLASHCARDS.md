5. What are common classification levels in government?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Unclassified, confidential, secret, and top secret. </details>

6. What role ensures security controls are applied correctly?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> The data custodian. </details>

7. Who is responsible for ensuring security policies are enforced on data?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> The security administrator. </details>

8. What is the main responsibility of a data owner?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Classifying data and determining access policies. </details>

9. How does data classification impact data retention policies?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Higher classification levels often require stricter retention and disposal policies. </details>

10. What are the five stages of the data lifecycle?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Creation, storage, usage, sharing, and disposal. </details>

11. What is data at rest?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Data stored on a physical or cloud storage medium. </details>

12. What is data in transit?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Data moving between systems or across networks. </details>

13. What is data in use?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Data actively being processed in memory or applications. </details>

14. What control is used to protect data in transit?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Encryption (e.g., TLS, IPsec) </details>

15. Make 2 Examples of controls used to protect data at rest?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Full disk encryption or database encryption. </details>

16. What does data remanence refer to?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Residual data remaining after deletion. </details>

17. How can organizations prevent unauthorized data access during disposal?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Secure deletion, shredding, or degaussing. </details>

18. What is the purpose of a data retention policy?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> To specify how long data should be kept before disposal. </details>

19. What regulation heavily influences data retention policies in healthcare?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> HIPAA </details>

20. What is tokenization?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Replacing sensitive data with a non-sensitive equivalent. </details>

21. What is masking in data security?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Hiding parts of data to protect sensitive information. </details>

22. How does hashing help in data security?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> It converts data into a fixed-length output that cannot be reversed. </details>

23. What is the purpose of data loss prevention (DLP) tools?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> To detect and prevent unauthorized data transfers. </details>

24. What is the main difference between symmetric and asymmetric encryption?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Symmetric encryption uses one key; asymmetric uses two (public/private). </details>

25. What is homomorphic encryption?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> A method that allows computation on encrypted data without decrypting it. </details>

26. How does a digital rights management (DRM) system protect data?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> By controlling access and usage of digital assets. </details>

27. What is an example of an access control model that protects data?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Role-based access control (RBAC). </details>

28. What type of access control is based on security labels and classifications?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Mandatory Access Control (MAC). </details>

29. What is personally identifiable information (PII)?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Information that can identify an individual, like name or SSN. </details>

30. What does GDPR stand for?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> General Data Protection Regulation. </details>

31. What is the main focus of HIPAA?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Protecting healthcare-related personal information (PHI). </details>

32. What is data minimization?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Collecting only the data necessary for a specific purpose. </details>

33. What is the right to be forgotten?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> GDPR provision allowing individuals to request data deletion. </details>

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> A high-level document outlining security expectations and responsibilities. </details>

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Unauthorized use of IT resources or applications. </details>

40. What is the biggest insider threat to data security?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> Employees mishandling sensitive information. </details>

41. What is the main goal of asset security?

<details> <summary>Show answer</summary> To protect information assets throughout their lifecycle. </details>
